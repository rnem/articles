In March 2006, a physicist walked into an Amazon building in Seattle to test something called S3. He walked out with his head spinning—suddenly, he had infinite storage at his fingertips without buying a single server.

That moment changed everything.

Before Amazon Web Services (AWS), launching a startup meant gambling tens of thousands of dollars on hardware you might not need. You had to forecast perfectly or risk missing your growth moment—or worse, not surviving long enough to reach it.

Today, I watch healthcare systems, financial institutions, and enterprises around the world build solutions that impact millions of lives, focusing on innovation instead of building and operating their own data centers.

From one service to over 240 and counting. From solving where to store data to enabling agentic AI that reasons and executes complex tasks. From three beta customers to millions worldwide. The through line remains constant: 𝗶𝗻𝗳𝗿𝗮𝘀𝘁𝗿𝘂𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗿𝗲 𝘀𝗵𝗼𝘂𝗹𝗱 𝗻𝗲𝘃𝗲𝗿 𝗯𝗲 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗯𝗮𝗿𝗿𝗶𝗲𝗿 𝘁𝗼 𝗮 𝗴𝗿𝗲𝗮𝘁 𝗶𝗱𝗲𝗮.

Fun AWS trivia: an unsolved math problem sparked it, an MBA intern became CEO (Matt Garman), early ideas were debated in a Seattle pub, Amazon EC2 was built in Cape Town by just eight engineers, and the AWS blog launched before the services themselves—written for 20 years by Jeff Barr

As an Enterprise TAM supporting customers in their journeys, I'm incredibly proud to be part of the team helping millions turn their boldest ideas into reality. But honestly? I'm most excited for what comes next.
